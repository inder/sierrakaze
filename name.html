Sierra Kaze

see-EH-rah KAH-zeh

The Name

Sierra

The Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. Represents strength, permanence, and the natural beauty of the West Coast.

Kaze 風

Japanese for "wind." Represents movement, change, and the invisible force that shapes landscapes over time.

The Meaning

Sierra Kaze is "Wind of the Sierra" — the mountain breeze that flows through California's peaks and valleys.

It captures the spirit of building things that last (like mountains) while staying adaptive and forward-moving (like wind). A blend of California roots and Japanese philosophy.

Written in Japanese

シエラ・カゼ Phonetic spelling (katakana)
風 The kanji character for wind

Why It Fits

A product studio grounded in Silicon Valley — always building, always evolving, shaping the future one product at a time.
